Data Critique

What information is included in our dataset?

Our team decided to look at the information in New York City and London. Both of the data sets include the following:

    • Listing information: Unique Identifier, Name, Description, Property Profile (URL), Neighborhood Overview, Geographic Latitude & Longitude, Property Type, Room Types, accommodates (Max Number of Guests), Room Numbers, Amenities, Price, Number of Nights, Min & Max number of nights required, and Availability.
    • Host Information: Host URL (Profile), Name, Since joined Airbnb, Host Location, About, Response Time, Response Rate, Acceptance Rate, if the Host is a SuperHost, Thumbnail URL, Host Neighborhood, Listing Count, Total Listing Count, Verifications, If the host has a profile picture, and If their identity is verified. 
    • Neighborhood Information: Neighborhood Cleansed and Neighborhood group cleansed. In New York, the neighborhood was the borough of the city which was then further broken down into smaller neighborhoods within each borough. In London, there are no boroughs, but the neighborhoods include different parts of Greater London.
    • Reviews and Ratings: Number of reviews in the last 12 months, last 30 days, first and last review. Additionally, the data set includes Rating, Accuracy, Cleanliness, Check-in, Communication, Location, and Value. 

What information, events, or phenomena our dataset can illuminate?

Listing information: Our dataset can illuminate information gathered over the span of 10 years, revealing which neighborhoods are more likely to be desired by customers. We can determine this by examining the most frequently booked Airbnb listings, analyzing reviews, and assessing the availability of the properties. Additionally, we can identify the types of rental units that are most frequently booked on Airbnb, providing insights into the preferences of travelers. The dataset allows us to gain insights into the extent to which Airbnb impacts factors such as housing, local economies, and tourism in these cities. Furthermore, it offers information on how persuasive or credible an owner appears through their profile and reviews from other renters, considering their years of experience with the platform/service.

In terms of events, the dataset can reveal the peak number of rooms rented within an area, irrespective of cost, depending on events such as concerts, ceremonies, popularity of tourist attractions, holidays, etc. Moreover, we can identify and compare the distinctive ways in which London and New York City are affected by Airbnb, recognizing the impacts. Another example of information revealed through our dataset is how the prices of Airbnb rooms have increased over time based on the economy of the country they are in, reflecting the quality of the service and the overall economy.

What our dataset cannot reveal?

Our group aims to examine sociocultural and socioeconomic factors across the Airbnb markets in New York and London. We are particularly interested in understanding how affordability and the cost of living impact specific sociocultural and socioeconomic aspects in these two cities. While we have gathered information on variables such as the average income of guests, the cost of staying at listings, and the length of stay, it would be beneficial to delve deeper into the cost of maintaining listings, extraneous fees that hosts might face, and the potential passive income generated by hosts from their listings. Moreover, there is limited information available on socio-cultural contributions. Exploring the demographics of both guests and hosts for the properties could provide valuable insights.

How the data was generated?

The datasets were generated via web scraping from Airbnb’s website. The Inside Airbnb team utilizes automated systems to collect a snapshot of available listings at the time. Subsequently, the team cleans, verifies, and compiles this snapshot with previously collected data from Airbnb’s website.

What are the original sources?

The data was scraped from publicly available information on Airbnb’s website by the Inside Airbnb team. Inside Airbnb is an independent investigative website founded by Murray Cox, utilizing in-house tools to collect and analyze the data. Currently, the Inside Airbnb team compiles data from over 90 cities. The specific data examined in this project includes the London dataset, last scraped in September 2023, and the New York dataset, last scraped in October 2023. The mission driving ‘Inside Airbnb’ is rooted in providing open-access data that aims to quantify and shed light on the multifaceted impact of short-term rentals within the context of housing and residential communities.

Who or what organization funded the creation of the dataset?

Our dataset comes from InsideAirbnb, a data collection website that stores information for listings from around the world. The datasets we are examining are focused on the New York and London markets. InsideAirbnb offers partnerships, collaborations, and requests donations to keep the site running.

What information is left out of the spreadsheet?

One thing that is left out of the spreadsheet is the reviewer’s history of the Airbnbs they may have rented and how satisfied they were with the owner and the services provided. This information could potentially be effective in communicating the overall quality that the renter is expecting from this service and the reasons why they chose the rooms they did, while also influencing their review based on any previous experiences they can compare it with. Another aspect omitted from the spreadsheet is the reason Airbnb is being rented in the first place. The renter could have used the Airbnb as a resting place, a meeting spot for friends, or during a business trip, and whether they adhered to the owner’s rules.

If our dataset were our only source, what information would be left out?

“Inside Airbnb” serves as our primary data source and plays a pivotal role in aggregating and structuring the dataset that we use. This organization is dedicated to making information freely available, enabling researchers, policymakers, and the public to better understand the ramifications of short-term rentals in various cities. The dataset’s focus on property and pricing information may oversimplify the socioeconomic context within which Airbnb operates. It may not capture the nuances and complexities of local economies and housing markets accurately. Additionally, without incorporating local community voices, the dataset cannot reflect the full spectrum of opinions and concerns regarding Airbnb’s presence in neighborhoods.
