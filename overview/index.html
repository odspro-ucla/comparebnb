Sourcing

Exploring the impact of Airbnb’s operations on the socioeconomic aspects of New York City and London is our primary research question. We aim to uncover the key differences between these two cities regarding Airbnb’s influence.

Our investigation delves into Airbnb’s role in shaping the housing markets of New York City and Central London, emphasizing its socioeconomic implications. To guide our research, we’ve selected primarily scholarly journals and credible national news articles as our sources. The scope of our readings extends beyond the specific contexts of New York City and London, encompassing material on Airbnb’s presence in other cities and research on issues such as segregation, discrimination, and gentrification. For instance, one source highlighted the rapid increase in house values and rent prices, accompanied by a loss of social capital in Central London, home to diverse communities (Boon et al., 2019).

Furthermore, shifting focus to legal aspects, Hofmann discusses the legal battle between Airbnb and New York City, shedding light on the government’s data requirements and the potential impact on privacy rights. Morris and Jorden provide narratives on the ongoing conflict between Airbnb and NYC, detailing new laws and regulations affecting short-term rentals. This type of research sheds light on the socioeconomic challenges linked to the expansion of short-term rentals through platforms like Airbnb, providing a contextual foundation for drawing connections.

During the research of this project, all comments on the YouTube recordings of the hearings were deliberately removed. On November 2, 2023, YouTube comments were still available, but by December 2, 2023, all YouTube comments had been deleted from the recordings of the hearings. We had earlier verified the page â€œRegistration and Requirements for Short-Term Rentalsâ€ from the site NYC Rules contained a comments section that had archived the deleted YouTube comments (2023). This mass deletion of public comments prompted our raised concerns of intentional silencing, so we scraped the text from the archived comments for future analysis.

We have curated a diverse array of secondary sources that go beyond city-specific details, delving into Airbnb as a corporation and its overarching challenges. This inclusive strategy enables us to weave together concepts from various sources alongside Airbnb datasets for both New York City and London, enhancing the depth and breadth of our research.

Processing

In the data processing stage, we found the Airbnb dataset from “Inside Airbnb” to be well-maintained, displaying minimal typos, errors, duplicates, or misleading titles. Our primary focus was refining the data for targeted analysis in New York City. After downloading the .csv file, we gained access to a comprehensive list of listings within the city. The organized structure based on neighborhoods greatly facilitated our task of pinpointing New York City listings.

Throughout the process of cleaning our data, we utilized Excel to ensure that all the data was neat and organized for further use and analysis. Presentation

Our findings are presented on a user-friendly and navigable website hosted on WordPress, generously supported by the University of California, Los Angeles. Initially, we considered mirroring the design of the Airbnb website but opted for a professional, clean aesthetic to improve the user experience. We employed a consistent theme, clear navigation, and a simple drop-down menu bar to enhance accessibility.

Visualizations, crafted using Tableau and Voyant Tools, are seamlessly embedded into the website, providing an interactive experience for users. To ensure maximum accessibility for all viewers, our Tableau visualizations utilize Tableau’s color-blind palette.

For our timeline, we utilized the open-source tool TimelineJS to create an interactive and engaging time-lapse of Airbnb’s history. This feature allows viewers to gain background information about the company. The timeline also sheds light on events that influenced specific populations and locations, such as New York and London, providing viewers with insight into the issues covered within our narrative.
